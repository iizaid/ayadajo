# Ayadajo — Go-To-Market Plan

Founder-led, high-touch, manual — the right approach for a focused vertical SaaS in Jordan. Goal: **first 3 pilots → first 5 paying clinics → repeatable motion.**

---

## Finding dental clinics in Jordan

### Instagram (primary channel — dental clinics market heavily here)
- Search/explore: `عيادة اسنان`, `طبيب اسنان`, `تجميل اسنان`, `زراعة اسنان`, `تقويم اسنان`, plus city tags: `عمان`, `اربد`, `الزرقاء`, `عمان اسنان`, `#اسنان_الاردن`.
- Look for: active posting, a real clinic (not just a personal doctor brand), location in Jordan, a booking phone/WhatsApp in bio, before/after posts, replies to DMs.
- Collect: handle, clinic name, city, phone/WhatsApp, follower range, posting activity, number of doctors (if visible).

### Google Maps (high-intent, verifiable)
- Search "dental clinic" / "عيادة اسنان" by city/area. Note: name, rating, review count, phone, website, hours, busy-ness signals.
- Reviews reveal pain (waiting times, no-shows, scheduling complaints) — great demo hooks.

### Findly / lead tools
- Use to scrape/enrich a structured list (name, phone, IG, Maps URL, city) and dedupe — saves manual collection time. Feed into a simple CRM sheet/Notion/Airtable with status columns.

### Other
- Referrals from pilot clinics (best source). Dental supply reps, dental Facebook/WhatsApp groups, dental conferences/expos in Jordan.

## Prioritizing clinics (lead scoring)
Score each on:
- **Pain signals** (reviews mention scheduling/no-shows; visibly busy).
- **Reachability** (responsive on IG/WhatsApp; bio has contact).
- **Fit** (small–mid clinic, 1–4 chairs; owner-operated; not already on heavy software).
- **Influence** (well-known locally → good reference/testimonial).
- **Tech-openness** (modern IG presence, online booking attempts).
Prioritize high pain + high reachability + good reference value for the **first cohort**.

## Ideal early adopter profile
- Owner-dentist who answers their own WhatsApp.
- 1–3 doctors, 1–2 reception staff, decent daily volume.
- Currently on paper/Excel/WhatsApp and *feels* the chaos.
- Active on Instagram (values modern image).
- Open to trying things, willing to give feedback, likely to refer.
- **Avoid for cohort 1:** very large clinics with existing software, or tiny low-volume clinics with no pain.

## Outreach workflow
1. **Warm the profile:** follow them, like/genuinely engage with a couple of posts (not spammy).
2. **First DM/WhatsApp** (short, Arabic, value-led — see OUTREACH_MESSAGES.md). Lead with a *clickable demo link or 60-sec video*, not a wall of text.
3. **Qualify:** if interested, ask 2–3 quick questions (how do you manage appointments today? how many no-shows?).
4. **Offer a short demo** (15 min, screen-share or in person).
5. Track every contact in the CRM sheet with status + next action + date.

## Follow-up workflow
- No reply in 2–3 days → **one** gentle follow-up (different angle, still short). Then stop (don't spam) — leave the door open.
- Interested but busy → propose 2 concrete demo times.
- After demo → follow up same day with a recap + trial offer.
- Always have a clear single **next step** and a date.

## Demo workflow
- 15-min default (see DEMO_SCRIPT.md). Show Today's Schedule → add patient → book → patient profile → reminder → owner dashboard. Tie each to *their* pain.
- End by offering to **create their trial on the spot**.

## Trial activation workflow
- Create the clinic (Super Admin, <2 min) during/after the demo.
- Do the **onboarding** (see CLINIC_ONBOARDING_PLAYBOOK.md): set hours/services/doctors, import a few patients, book a real appointment together, send a test reminder.
- Set expectations: "Use it for your real day this week; I'll check in."

## Trial-to-paid conversion workflow
- **Day 1:** onboard + first real appointment booked.
- **Day 2–3:** check in (WhatsApp) — any blockers? watch usage.
- **Day 6–7:** mid-trial call — show them their own numbers (appointments, no-shows, collected).
- **Day 10–11:** address objections; present the plan + price; offer yearly.
- **Day 13–14:** convert — share CliQ/bank details, record payment, activate. If not ready, offer a short extension once.
- Make the value concrete: "Here's what you tracked in 2 weeks; here's the price; here's how to pay."

## Weekly sales routine (solo founder)
- **Mon:** add 15–20 new leads (IG/Maps/Findly); plan the week's outreach.
- **Tue–Wed:** send first messages + follow-ups; book demos.
- **Thu:** run demos; activate trials; onboard.
- **Fri:** check in on active trials; convert; collect feedback; update CRM + metrics.
- Daily: respond to interested clinics fast (speed wins).

## Metrics to track
- Leads added · messages sent · reply rate · demos booked · demos done · trials started · **trial→paid conversion %** · time-to-onboard · paying clinics · MRR · churn · referrals · testimonials collected.
- Watch the funnel: where do leads drop? (no reply? demo no-show? trial not converting?) Fix the weakest stage.

## Handling objections (common ones)
- **"We use paper/WhatsApp and it's fine."** → "It works — until a double-booking or a forgotten patient. Try it free for 2 weeks on your real day; if it doesn't save you time, drop it."
- **"Too expensive."** → re-anchor on no-show cost + time saved; offer yearly; never just slash price.
- **"No time to learn it."** → "I'll set it up for you and train your receptionist in 30 minutes."
- **"Is my patient data safe?"** → explain isolation, private files, backups, audit (a strength).
- **"We tried software before, too complicated."** → "That's exactly why we built Ayadajo simple and only for dental clinics — your receptionist will get it in an hour."
- **"Send me info."** → send a short recap + demo link + offer a time; follow up.

## How to avoid sounding spammy
- Personalize (use clinic name; reference something real).
- Short, respectful, value-first; one clear ask.
- One follow-up max if ignored; never blast identical messages.
- Lead with help, not a pitch; Arabic that sounds human, not corporate.

## Collecting feedback
- Weekly WhatsApp check-ins during trials; a 10-min call mid-trial.
- Ask: what's confusing? what's missing? what would make you pay? what did you stop using paper for?
- Log feedback in QUESTIONS_AND_ASSUMPTIONS.md / an issues list; close the loop ("you asked for X, here it is").

## Getting the first testimonial
- After a clinic has a "win" (a recovered no-show, a smooth day, clear numbers), ask: "Would you share a short sentence on how Ayadajo helped?" + permission to use their name/logo.
- Offer a referral reward (free month) for introductions.
- Turn testimonials + screenshots into landing-page and outreach proof.
