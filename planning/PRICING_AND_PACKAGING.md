# Ayadajo — Pricing & Packaging

> All JOD numbers are **recommended starting hypotheses** to validate with the first clinics, not fixed facts. Currency: **JOD**. Billing: **monthly or yearly**, manual payment (CliQ / bank transfer / cash) in V1.

Pricing philosophy: **price on value (no-shows saved + time saved), not on cost.** A clinic losing 4 no-shows/week at ~20–40 JOD each is losing hundreds of JOD monthly; a 30–60 JOD tool that recovers even a fraction is an easy yes. **Avoid underpricing** — too-cheap signals "toy," attracts price-shoppers, and starves support capacity.

## Plans overview

| Plan | Target clinic | Price (monthly) | Price (yearly) | Doctors | Staff users | Reminders | Support |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Pilot** | First 3–5 reference clinics | Free for 1–2 months, then discounted | — | up to 3 | up to 5 | manual WA + email | high-touch (founder) |
| **Starter** | Solo / 1-doctor clinic | **35 JOD** | **350 JOD** (~2 mo free) | 1 | up to 3 | manual WA links + email | standard |
| **Pro** | Small clinic (2–3 doctors) | **60 JOD** | **600 JOD** | up to 3 | up to 8 | manual WA + email + (later) automated email | priority |
| **Plus** | Busy / multi-doctor clinic | **95 JOD** | **950 JOD** | up to 6 | up to 15 | all of Pro + early access to automation | priority + onboarding help |

> These are *anchors*. If clinics balk, the problem is usually value communication, not price — don't reflexively discount. Validate with the first 5 conversations (ask "what would feel fair?").

### 14-day trial
- Full V1 feature access, real data, 14 days, auto-created with the clinic.
- No card required (manual billing). Banner shows days left; nudge at ≤3 days.
- Extendable by AtlasJo (+7d) for promising leads.
- Goal of the trial: get them to **run a real day** and **see their numbers** — that's what converts.

### Pilot plan (first 3–5 clinics)
- **Free for 1–2 months**, then a **locked discounted rate** (e.g. 50% off Starter/Pro for the first 12 months) **in exchange for:** honest weekly feedback, a testimonial, and permission to reference them.
- These clinics are your design partners and proof. Treat them like gold; over-support them.
- Cap pilots at **≤3 concurrent** (solo-founder support limit).

- **Extra doctors/users:** add-on (e.g. +10 JOD/month per extra doctor) rather than forcing a tier jump — reduces friction.

## Setup fee options
- **Default: no setup fee** for pilots and early clinics (reduce friction, you want adoption + testimonials).
- **Optional "Done-for-you onboarding"** add-on (e.g. **30–50 JOD one-time**): AtlasJo imports their patient list, configures hours/services/doctors, and trains staff. Sell this as convenience, not a barrier. Waive for pilots.
- Once you have proof + demand, you can introduce a modest setup fee to filter for serious clinics.

---

## First 3–5 clinics strategy
1. Hand-pick clinics likely to give great feedback and become references (engaged owners, real pain, decent volume).
2. Free pilot → intensive onboarding (you do the setup) → weekly check-ins → fix blockers fast.
3. Lock them into a **founder rate** they keep as long as they stay (loyalty + word-of-mouth).
4. Convert their success into **testimonials, screenshots, and referrals** — your best sales asset.
5. Use their feedback to harden V1 before wider outreach.

## Discount strategy (disciplined)
- **Yearly discount:** ~2 months free (the main legitimate discount; improves cash + retention).
- **Founder/pilot rate:** locked discount for the first cohort — framed as partnership, time-limited offer.
- **Referral reward:** a free month for a clinic that refers a paying clinic.
- **Avoid ad-hoc discounting** to close deals — it erodes price integrity and trains clinics to haggle. If price is the objection, re-sell value or offer yearly, not a random cut.

## When to increase prices
- After the first ~10 paying clinics + testimonials + a few fast-follow features (automated reminders, tooth chart, files maturity).
- Grandfather existing clinics (especially pilots) at their rate; raise for **new** clinics.
- Raise when support load and value clearly justify it — communicate added value, not just a higher number.

## How to communicate pricing
- **Anchor on value first:** "How many no-shows do you have a week? What does each cost you?" Then the price feels small.
- Lead with **one clear recommended plan** (Pro for most clinics), not a confusing menu.
- Be transparent: simple monthly/yearly, no hidden fees, cancel anytime, your data is yours.
- Offer **yearly** as the "best value" once they're convinced monthly.
- Quote in **JOD**, plainly. Mention manual payment (CliQ/transfer/cash) as easy and local.

## How to handle manual payment
- After trial, AtlasJo shares CliQ/bank details; clinic pays; AtlasJo records it (`subscription_payments`) and activates.
- Send a simple subscription **receipt/invoice**.
- Renewal reminders (email) before period end; record renewal payment; extend period.
- Keep every payment recorded in-system (single source of truth), never a side spreadsheet.

## How to avoid underpricing
- Don't anchor to "cheapest tool" — anchor to **value delivered** (no-shows recovered, time saved, owner visibility).
- Factor in your **support cost** (high-touch onboarding isn't free).
- Starter at ~35 JOD, not 10 — a 10 JOD tool can't fund good support and signals low quality.
- If unsure, price slightly higher and offer a generous trial; it's easier to discount than to raise.

## Positioning vs generic ERP tools (without attacking competitors)
- Don't bash competitors. Position by **fit**: "Generic business/ERP tools can do a lot, but they're built for many industries and can feel heavy. Ayadajo is built only for dental clinics in Jordan — Arabic-first, in the browser, simple enough for your receptionist on day one."
- Compete on **focus, simplicity, Arabic-first, local support, dental-specific**, not on having more features.
- Price comparably or slightly below heavy ERP suites while being obviously simpler and more relevant — value, not cheapness.
